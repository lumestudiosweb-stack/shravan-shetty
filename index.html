A Short Bio

Of bone, brackets & bytes.

D r. Shravan Shetty has spent over a decade at the intersection of clinical orthodontics, academic teaching, and digital workflow design. After completing his MDS at SDM College of Dental Sciences, Dharwad in 2016, he joined Manipal College of Dental Sciences, Mangalore — where he today serves as Assistant Professor in the Department of Orthodontics & Dentofacial Orthopaedics, mentoring both undergraduate and postgraduate students.

His clinical interest sits at the leading edge of orthodontic care: clear aligner therapy, lingual orthodontics, and biomechanics of treatment. His research focuses on micro-implant stability and the evolving evidence base for aligner-driven tooth movement. Beyond the clinic he is a regular speaker at masterclasses, contributes to institutional infection control governance, and has published in journals spanning Biomed & Pharmacology Journal, International Journal of Orthodontics and the Journal of Aligner Orthodontics.

Chapter 02 · The Researcher

01
June 2019
Biomed & Pharmacology Journal · 12(1): 341—346

A Comparative Evaluation of Reliability of an Android-based App & Computerised Cephalometric Tracing Program.

Shettigar P, Shetty S, Naik RD, Basavaraddi SM, Patil AK

"Only four of fifteen parameters showed statistically significant differences — the OneCeph app proves reliable and user-friendly for clinicians' day-to-day cephalometric analysis."

The study assessed an Android smartphone-based app (OneCeph) against the gold-standard Dolphin Imaging system, across 50 cephalometric radiographs with 20 landmarks and 15 skeletal, dental and soft-tissue parameters. Eleven of fifteen parameters showed strong positive correlation — establishing the smartphone app as a clinically viable tool for routine cephalometric work.

02
June 2019
Indian Journal of Public Health Research & Development · 10(4): 278—282

Digital India, Digitizing the Orthodontic Office — A Contemporary Patient Management System.

Jose NP, Shetty S, Naik DG, Shetty S, Desai A, Ashith MV, Mary L

"A paperless transition for an academic orthodontic department — scheduling, clinical records, imaging, and reporting unified into a single contemporary stack."

A practice report on the design and rollout of a contemporary patient management system inside an academic orthodontic department — covering scheduling, clinical records, imaging integration, and reporting. The paper outlines design choices, departmental workflow shifts, and lessons learned from migrating an established clinic onto a fully digital stack.
