02 · Research

Featured Research

Investigating the intersection of digital tools, orthodontic biomechanics, and clinical workflow.

June 2019

Biomed & Pharmacology Journal · 12(1), 341–346

A Comparative Evaluation of Reliability of an Android-based App and Computerized Cephalometric Tracing Program for Orthodontic Cephalometric Analysis

Shettigar P, Shetty S, Naik RD, Basavaraddi SM, Patil AK

This study assessed the reliability of the Android smartphone-based OneCeph app against Dolphin Imaging — the established gold-standard computerised cephalometric tracing program. Across 50 cephalometric radiographs, 20 landmarks and 15 skeletal, dental, and soft-tissue parameters were measured and compared by paired t-test. Only 4 of 15 parameters showed statistically significant differences (p < 0.05); the remaining 11 demonstrated strong positive correlation. The OneCeph app proved reliable, user-friendly, and clinically usable for day-to-day cephalometric analysis.

Indian Journal of Public Health Research & Development · 10(4), 278–282

Digital India, Digitizing the Orthodontic Office — Our Experience with Design and Implementation of a Contemporary Patient Management System

Jose NP, Shetty S, Naik DG, Shetty S, Desai A, Ashith MV, Mary L

A practice report on the design and rollout of a contemporary, paperless patient management system within an academic orthodontic department — covering scheduling, clinical records, imaging integration, and reporting. The paper outlines design choices, departmental workflow shifts, and lessons learned from migrating an established clinic onto a fully digital stack.
